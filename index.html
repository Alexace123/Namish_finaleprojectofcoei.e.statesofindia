SN. State Name Capital Mostly Spoken Language Culture
1. Madhya Pradesh Bhopal Hindi The culture of Madhya Pradesh is a harmonious
people of Jains, Buddhists, Hindu Christians and Sikhs.
Moreover, the tribal communities of the state
2. Karnataka Bengaluru Kannada The southern state of Karnataka, in India, has a distinct
art and culture informed by a long history of diverse linguistic
and religious ethnicity.
3. Gujrat Gandhinagar Gujrati Embracing different religious faiths,
Gujaratis demonstrate a vibrant mix of Hinduism,
Islam, Jainism, and Buddhism.
4. Uttar Pradesh Lucknow Hindi and Urdu The Culture of Uttar Pradesh is an Indian culture
which has its roots in Hindi and Urdu literature, music,
fine arts, drama and cinema.
5. Punjab Chandigarh Punjabi Punjab has one of the oldest and richest cultures of the world. Its diversity
and uniqueness is evident in the Punjabi poetry, philosophy, spirituality, education, artistry,
music, cuisine, science, technology etc.
6. Rajasthan Jaipur Rajasthani Folk songs are commonly ballads which relate heroic deeds
and love stories; and religious or devotional songs known as bhajans and banis {often accompanied by musical
instruments like dholak, sitar, sarangi etc.} are also sung.
7. Maharashtra Mumbai/Nagpur Marathi About 90% of Maharashtrians are Hindu. The Maharashtrian culture consists
of people from all a vast majority of religions.
8. Kerala Thiruvananthapuram {Trivandrum} Malayalam The culture of Kerala is a combination of both Indian and Dravidian culture.
Now days this culture is being influenced by the neighboring states as well.
9. West Bengal Kolkata {culcutta} Bengali The culture of West Bengal is an Indian culture
which has its roots in the Bengali literature,
music, fine arts, drama and cinema. Over here Durga puja is celebrated vastly.
10. Tamil Nadu Chennai Tamil Hinduism lies at the core of the culture of Tamil Nadu.
Among the most famous of the state's temples,
which number in the tens of thousands, are the 7th- and 8th-century structures at Mamallapura.
11. Tripura Agartala Bengali / English Tripura has several diverse ethno-linguistic groups,
which has given rise to a composite culture.
12. Odisha Bhubneshwar Odia Odisha is a confluence of the Aryan, Dravidian and Adivasi cultures.
Most festivals in the state bring out some part of these cultures and celebrate their religion by way
of festivals which span out the entire year.
13. Mizoram Aizawl Mizo/ English The culture of the Mizos is intrinsically woven with their practice of Jhuming cultivation.
14. Assam Dispur Assamese Known for its rich culture and diverse population,
the culture of Assam is a fusion of Indo Burmese, Mongolian and Aryan influences.
15. Manipur Imphal Manipuri In the field of art and culture, the state is best represented
by its classical and folk dance forms.
16. Nagaland Kohima Konyak Nagaland is known for its exquisite bamboo and cane products, weaving and wood carving.
The most important works of art and crafts exist in the form of Basketry, Weaving, Woodcarving,
Pottery, Metal work, Jewellery and Beadwork.
17. Bihar Patna Hindi Bihar has a rich cultural heritage.
The predominant themes are from the myths and legends of Hinduism.
18. Andhra Pradesh Amaravati Telugu The culture of Andhra Pradesh is an integral part
of the rich cultural heritage of the country.
19. Chhatisgarh Raipur Chhattisgarhi Chhattisgarh is rich in its cultural heritage.
The State has a very unique and vibrant culture.
20. Telangana Hyderabad Telugu Telangana State has long been a meeting place for
diverse languages and cultures.
21. Jharkhand Ranchi Hindi Traditional folk music, instruments and dances are the norm.
The area is known for the Dokra art form while festivals such as Basant Panchami, Holi, Diwali, Chath, Jityya
Bhaiya are celebrated here.
22. Uttarakhand Dehradun / Gairsain Hindi Uttarakhand has a 'Pahari' culture. Folk dance, music and festivals
are a huge part of Uttarakhand culture.
23. Sikkim Gangtok Nepali The people of Sikkim are warm, simple and friendly with a nature gaiety.
The customs and rituals of Sikkim are as diverse as the ethnic that inhabit the land
24. Arunachal Pradesh Itanagar English The culture of Arunachal Pradesh is truly varied in the sense
the state has 26 major tribes including sub-tribes.
25. Goa Panaji Konkani The culture of Goa is an amalgamation of Indian and Portuguese culture.
26. Himachal Pradesh Shimla / Dharmashala Hindi Himachal Pradesh is a multi-religion practising, multicultural and multilingual
state. Some of the most commonly spoken languages are Hindi and
the various Pahari languages.
27. Meghalaya Shillong English The people of Meghalaya are known to be hospitable, cheerful and friendly. Traditionally,
the Khasis believe that their religion is God given and is based
on the belief of one supreme God.
28. Haryana Chandigarh Haryanvi The people of Haryana strictly adhere to their customs and cultural traditions.
Yoga and chanting of Vedic mantras have become an innate part of their lifestyle. The dialect of Haryana,
popularly known as Haryanvi.
